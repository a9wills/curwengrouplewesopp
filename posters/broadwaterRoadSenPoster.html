Investment Case

Demand-led

01. Demand Pressure

West Sussex EHCPs have grown from c.3,400 in 2015 to 10,000+ in 2025, with specialist placement pressure rising.

02. Commissioner Logic

Out-of-county SEMH placements and high transport costs create a strong rationale for local specialist capacity.

03. Operator Appeal

Existing F1 use, campus scale, parking and vocational space support phased entry for a specialist provider.

SEN Suitability Checklist

Building + use case
  • Multi-building campus supports phased opening, separation of cohorts and specialist pathways.
  • Existing education use and vacant accommodation reduce the route to occupation.
  • Vocational workshops can support post-16 SEND, life-skills and employability training.
  • A24/A27 access and 132 spaces support staff, transport, minibus and visitor movement.
  • Safeguarding layout, outdoor provision and exact pupil capacity require operator review.

Key Risks

Needs checking
  • Final SEN type, pupil profile and capacity need operator-led validation.
  • Highways, minibus circulation, drop-off and safeguarding routes require testing.
  • Outdoor space, boundaries and secure recreation areas need physical inspection.
  • Capex, phasing, M&E condition and compliance costs must be confirmed.
  • Any planning, licensing or statutory education approvals need specialist advice.

Recommended Next Steps

  • Commission SEN operator walkthrough and floorplan capacity review.

  • Confirm highways, transport, parking and drop-off strategy.

  • Prepare full investor pack if operator feedback is positive.
