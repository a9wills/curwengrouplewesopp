01 - Executive Summary

A 12-unit sheltered housing setting with the right operational ingredients for sheltered housing.

Margaret Fisher House is a sheltered housing opportunity at 30 Old Kent Road, Paddock Wood, Tonbridge, Kent TN12 6JD. The property provides 12 self-contained units, four of which are configured as one-bedroom apartments.

Each room benefits from a wet room and cooking facilities. The two one-bedroom flats in the bungalow include comprehensive kitchen areas within the lounge, while the main building provides shared amenity and support facilities including a communal kitchen, lounge and garden area. 2 of the units are configured as one-bedrrom flats all be it below the minimum space standards to be classified as such.

The building is currently undergoing comprehensive refurbishment and is expected to be available in late 2026. Its secure access, staff bedroom, lift, laundry, parking and town-centre location make it particularly relevant for sheltered housing operators supporting residents who require privacy, independence, practical support and access to everyday services.

02 - Sheltered Housing Use Case

Privacy, independence and support within one managed setting.

For sheltered housing providers, Margaret Fisher House offers a practical balance between self-contained privacy and managed support. Residents can occupy independent rooms or flats with their own wet room and cooking facilities, while still having access to staff accommodation, shared amenities, a shared communal lounge and kitchen area, and secure site controls.

The layout supports a managed sheltered housing model where residents can choose privacy when needed, while communal space remains available for managed support, peer contact, life skills, family work and structured activity.

Operational suitability will remain subject to provider safeguarding protocols, risk assessment, commissioning requirements, security specification and any statutory approvals required for the intended use.
